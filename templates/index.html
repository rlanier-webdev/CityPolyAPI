About the Rivalry

The City-Poly football game is the oldest football rivalry in Maryland and the second-oldest public high school football rivalry in the United States. Since their first meeting in 1889 at Clifton Park in northeast Baltimore, Baltimore City College (the Black Knights) and Baltimore Polytechnic Institute (the Engineers) have clashed over 135 times — with only two missed years — 1902 and 2020 — in more than a century of play.

For generations, the game was a Thanksgiving Day institution, drawing tens of thousands of fans to Municipal Stadium and later Memorial Stadium on 33rd Street. The 1965 Thanksgiving Day matchup — played before a crowd of 25,000 with both teams undefeated — remains one of the most storied chapters in Baltimore high school sports history, ending in a decisive 52-6 City victory. In 1993, the game moved from Thanksgiving to the first Saturday of November, where it has been held ever since.

More than a football game, the City-Poly rivalry is a civic tradition. Generations of Baltimore families have alumni ties to one school or the other, and the annual contest draws city-wide attention far beyond the student bodies. This archive preserves the historical record of every game — scores, teams, and notes — from the first kickoff to the present day.
